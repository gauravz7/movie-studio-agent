# Camera Language: Emotion → Camera

Map the dramatic intent of a moment to concrete camera choices. Use as a starting
grammar, not a rulebook — adjust to the beat.

| Emotion / intent | Angle | Height | Lens | Movement |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Tension / threat | Low angle | Chest/below eye | Longer (85mm+) | Slow push-in |
| Vulnerability / powerlessness | High angle | Above eye | Wide | Static |
| Intimacy / connection | Eye level | Eye | Close, shallow DOF | Static or gentle |
| Awe / scale | Low | Low, near ground | Wide | Slow, sweeping |
| Neutral / information | Eye level | Eye | Medium (35-50mm) | Static |
| Unease / disorientation | Dutch (canted) | Varies | Wide-ish | Handheld / drift |
| Isolation / smallness | High / wide | High | Wide | Static, distant |
| Rising realization | Eye level | Eye | Medium-long | Slow push-in |
| Energy / chaos | Varies | Varies | Wide | Handheld / whip |
| Surveillance / detachment | High | High | Long | Locked-off or slow pan |

**Notes on the levers**

- **Angle**: low empowers the subject; high diminishes it; eye level is parity.
- **Height**: reciprocal heights in a reverse make two people share a look.
- **Lens**: longer focal length compresses space and isolates (intimacy, threat);
  wider exaggerates space and depth (scale, unease). Keep lens consistent across a
  shot/reverse pair.
- **Movement**: a slow push-in intensifies focus; static holds observe; handheld
  adds instability and immediacy.
