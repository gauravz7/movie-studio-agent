# Shot Pattern Library

Pick the coverage that serves the beat's intent. **Reuse** the anchors listed;
**vary** the camera as noted.

| Pattern | Use when | Anchors to REUSE | Camera to VARY |
|---|---|---|---|
| Establishing + coverage | Opening a scene; orienting the audience in space before going closer | Establishing frame defines the geography; all subsequent shots re-condition on it | Start wide, then progress to medium/close on the subjects |
| Shot/reverse-shot | A dialogue or confrontation between two subjects | Establishing frame + both character refs + the sibling shot's keyframe | Swap subject and reciprocal eyeline; keep lens and screen sides constant |
| POV | Showing exactly what a character sees | Establishing frame + the object/space seen; preceding shot of the character looking | Camera becomes the character's eyeline; height/lens match their gaze |
| Insert / cutaway | Emphasizing a detail (prop, hand, clock) or cutting away for rhythm/time | Canonical prop reference + the scene's lighting/time-of-day | Tight framing on the detail; often macro/close, static |
| Push-in | Rising tension, dawning realization, growing focus on one subject | Same subject, same setup as the wider shot it grows from | Hold angle/lens; move slowly closer to tighten the frame |
| Match cut | Bridging two spaces/times via a shared shape, motion, or graphic | The matched graphic/motion element across both shots | Compose the outgoing and incoming frames to align on the match |
| Montage | Compressing time or surveying many beats/locations quickly | Character/prop refs carried across fragments for coherence | Freely vary size, angle, and location shot to shot; unify by rhythm |
